Abstract

The objective of this project is to design a liquid-nitrogen cryo-fluorescence stage for super-resolution 3D fluorescence microscopy. The primary goal is maintaining biological samples at temperatures between -140°C and -196°C to avoid crystallization and ensure imaging quality. The developed cryostage design integrates six essential subsystems: thermal reservoir, thermal insulation, height adjustment, sample holder, data acquisition, and vibration isolation. The design prioritizes reducing vibrations, minimizing frost formation, and precisely maintaining temperature. Key design considerations include the precise control of working distance between the sample and objective lens, efficient thermal insulation, and robust vibration management.

A CAD model was created to optimize the interaction of subsystems, ensuring manufacturability, ease of use, and system compatibility with existing microscopy setups. The final concept uses lead screws for height adjustment, a copper-based thermal reservoir for efficient cooling, and a silicone funnel for thermal insulation and vapor management. Machining processes combine CNC milling and 3D printing, balancing cost efficiency with precision fabrication. This cryostage concept ensures effective thermal isolation, enhances imaging stability, and meets project constraints including a $3000 budget.
